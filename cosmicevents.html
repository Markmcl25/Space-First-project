Cosmic events are extraordinary occurrences in the vast expanse of the universe that captivate our imagination and deepen our understanding of the cosmos. These events encompass a diverse array of phenomena, each contributing to the beauty and complexity of the universe. Here are brief descriptions of some common cosmic events:


Supernova: A supernova is a powerful and explosive event that occurs when a massive star reaches the end of its life cycle. During a supernova, the star releases an immense amount of energy, outshining an entire galaxy for a brief period.


Solar Eclipse: A solar eclipse happens when the Moon passes between the Sun and Earth, blocking all or part of the Sun's light. This creates a temporary shadow on Earth, casting a breathtaking display of darkness during the day.


Lunar Eclipse: A lunar eclipse occurs when the Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, causing the Earth's shadow to cover the Moon. This phenomenon results in a reddish hue known as a "blood moon" due to Earth's atmosphere filtering and refracting sunlight.


Asteroid Impact: Asteroid impacts involve the collision of space rocks with planets or moons, causing significant changes to the celestial body's surface. These events have played a crucial role in shaping the geology and history of planets.


Black Hole: A black hole is an extremely dense region in space where gravity is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape its grasp. Black holes are formed from the remnants of massive stars undergoing gravitational collapse.
